# Product Planning

## Purpose
Reusable procedure to execute product planning tasks with consistent quality.

## When to use
When a task directly or indirectly affects this domain.

## When not to use
When the task has no impact on this domain or a more specific skill exists.

## Inputs
- Goal.
- Scope.
- Related files.
- Business rules.
- Constraints.
- Expected risk.

## Procedure
1. Read PRD, rules, architecture, and security guidelines.
2. Confirm scope and dependencies.
3. Evaluate alternatives.
4. Choose the simplest and most reversible option.
5. Implement or review.
6. Validate positive and negative cases.
7. Assess risks.
8. Update documentation.
9. Deliver evidence.

## Acceptance Criteria
- Correct.
- Secure.
- Understandable.
- Maintainable.
- Tested.
- Documented.
- No premature complexity.

## Escalation
High risk: specialized review.
Critical risk: halt and block.
